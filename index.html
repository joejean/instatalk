Our Product

User Experience
 
 
 
 
TalkMe’s breakthough is impressive not only from the perspective of computer science but also from the perspective of user experience design.
The end-user (Speaker A) simply puts the TalkMe earbud in their ear, and holds out his or her mobile phone in the direction of the individual with whom they are having a conversation (Speaker B).
The mobile phone of Speaker A receives the sound input of both Speaker A and Speaker B. When Speaker A speaks, the mobile phone translates his speech and plays it out loud to Speaker B in Speaker B’s language and in Speaker A’s voice. When Speaker B speaks, the mobile phone translates his speech and plays it for Speaker A in Speaker A’s earbud. By playing Speaker B’s speech through Speaker A’s earbud, sounds made by the mobile phone are not confused as they are only Speaker A’s words.
How does it work?
 
 
Instead of simply matching standard inputs with a library of outputs, TalkMe uses deep neural networks to recognize a variety of speech accents, patterns, and speeds with increased accuracy.
Deep neural networks (DNNs) work by a compiling a large number of independent inputs in order to make a single decision. By feeding the DNNs a variety examples and giving them feedback on whether or not their classification of a word was correct, the DNNs then get a better understanding of what combination of inputs yield the correct word and improve over time.
 
FAQ: How is TalkMe used if only one user has the app?
 
 
You may envision yourself in a situation where your listener is not using the TalkMe app, but you still urgently need to communicate with this individual.
Imagine, you are in Spain ordering coffee, and the waiter asks you, “Como queires el café?” You have the TalkMe app on your phone, and the app translates the words in the waiter’s voice to, “How would you like your coffee?” However, you cannot respond in your native tongue, as the waiter is not using TalkMe. You do not speak Spanish.
What do you do? In this situation, TalkMe has a solution. TalkMe will contextually provide suggestions to words and phrases you might want to say. The app will play in your ear a set of words in the target language combined with words in your native language.
In your own voice, TalkMe will say “If you want sugar, say ‘con azúcar,’ if you do not want sugar, say ‘sin azúcar,’ if you want milk say, ‘con leche,’ if you do not want milk say, ‘café solo.’”
TalkMe comes preprogrammed to perform this function in a wide variety of situations. You can then pick the option you want, communicate with the waiter, and successfully complete your conversation!
